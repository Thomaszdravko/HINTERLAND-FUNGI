Tour Information

Explore stunning biodiversity across a range of ecosystems in the Gold Coast Hinterland. Our tours run for approximately 2.5 hours, and in that time, you will learn about the ecology of fungi, how to identify a variety of species in the field, and how to be a citizen scientist contributing to our understanding of Australian taxonomy.

Learn how to search for wild mushrooms, including prized edible species (please note collection of fungi is not allowed on the tour). Receive tips for photography and participate in a Q&A session where you can access local knowledge and get answers to all of your mushroom-related questions. Participants receive a complimentary booklet titled An Introduction to Mushroom Identification.

Bio

Thomas Zdravko is a fungi expert and educator based in the Gold Coast Hinterland. Passionate about building public awareness of fungi and their important role in the ecosystem, Thomas facilitates workshops, presentations, lectures, and guided forays. He specialises primarily in the identification and taxonomy of native Australian fungi, as well as the foraging of edible species.

Thomas is the founder and an administrator of Australia & New Zealand Fungus Identification, the largest fungi identification Facebook group in Australia, with over 60,000 members.

About

Fungi Field Notes is designed to help beginners, seasoned field researchers, and everyone in between by replacing paper observation sheets with a convenient digital observation tool. It guides users step by step through the process of documenting the macro characteristics and supporting information needed for a proper scientific description, then automatically generates either a detailed list description or a polished written description that can be saved to a foray log or exported to iNaturalist.

Because the information is recorded in a clear, structured way, it is far easier to compare against fungal keys and reference material than rough handwritten notes or photo-only observations. It is useful for mycological societies and forays, as an educational tool, or simply for the fungi nerd who wants to level up their observation skills. Because so many fungi remain undocumented or poorly documented, this kind of structured field data can be extremely valuable.

You can also save Fungi Field Notes to your phone like an app for quick access in the field.

On iPhone or iPad: open it in Safari, tap the Share button, then choose Add to Home Screen.
On Android: open it in Chrome, tap the menu, then choose Add to Home screen or Install app.

Fungi Field Notes is currently in beta testing, and we are keen for community feedback. If you notice bugs, missing features, awkward wording, or have ideas for improvement, we would love to hear from you.
